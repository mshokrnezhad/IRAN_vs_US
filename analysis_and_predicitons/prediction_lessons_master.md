# Prediction Lessons Master

## How To Use

- Read this file before writing each new weekly pattern analysis and prediction.
- Append new lessons only when they are specific and reusable.
- Do not repeat the same lesson in slightly different wording.

## Lessons

### 2026-03-07 to 2026-03-13

- Do not treat leadership uncertainty only as forecast fog; in wartime, rapid succession resolution can become the defining event of the week and must be modeled as its own branch.
- Separate escalation logic from escalation mechanism. A correct call that pressure will widen is not enough if the forecast over-specifies the wrong actor or theater as the main instrument.
- When maritime disruption is already active, do not assume Iraqi or other land-based proxy activation will be the next most likely widening path; shipping and Gulf infrastructure may remain the preferred lower-cost pressure channel.
- Add a regime-resilience check whenever public rhetoric focuses on imminent collapse. Intelligence assessments, visible command continuity, and public appearances can invalidate collapse narratives even during intense bombardment.
- Treat emergency energy-policy responses as forecastable behavior inside the conflict window. Strategic reserve releases, sanctions waivers, and oil-flow management are part of the weekly operating picture, not just downstream consequences.
- Use a more granular proxy-front scale: latent, active-bounded, active-expanding, and full-surge. This reduces the risk of calling a front "bounded" when it is already moving toward expansion.
- Do not infer real diplomatic progress from general calls for restraint or mediator activity unless battlefield behavior also narrows in the same window.
- When a forecast assigns only medium confidence to a structural pattern that has repeated across consecutive days, consider whether the label is overly cautious and should be raised.

### 2026-03-14 to 2026-03-20

- Track coalition solicitation separately from coalition formation. Public U.S. calls for allies to help secure Hormuz are not evidence that a real operational coalition is about to materialize.
- Do not assume U.S.-Israeli battlefield alignment means identical war aims. Forecast target-priority and end-state divergence explicitly, especially when energy infrastructure and postwar regional order enter the discussion.
- When public U.S. messaging mixes victory or wind-down language with new deployments, funding requests, and continued strikes, weight the operational indicators more heavily than the rhetoric.
- In a high-attribution-fog maritime environment, forecast the disruptive function first and keep actor attribution conditional; elevated shipping risk does not require a clearly demonstrated Houthi-centered mechanism in every week.
- Add a U.S. domestic-legitimacy check to weekly forecasts when intelligence dissent, senior-official resignations, or public threat-assessment disputes appear. Internal political fracture can become a real driver of war durability inside the forecast window.

### 2026-03-21 to 2026-03-27

- Model Hormuz with a middle state between open passage and formal closure. Selective permission, humanitarian carveouts, and toll-like access control can be the real coercive mechanism.
- Treat deadline extensions and public claims of `productive contacts` as bargaining behavior, not de-escalation evidence, unless strike tempo or shipping access actually loosens.
- Keep threshold-crossing risk separate from the weekly baseline. A forecast can be right about endurance-war continuity and still miss long-range strikes on distant bases or new classes of sensitive targets.
- Separate intelligence-validated attrition from public victory rhetoric. Damage claims can outrun confirmed battlefield effects and distort confidence in the next forecast.
- When Israel is militarily aligned with Washington but publicly outside the diplomatic track, raise the probability that target categories will widen faster than negotiations can constrain them.
- Forecast proxy actors with two fields: current operational role and latent entry conditions. This helps when a group like the Houthis is not yet the main mechanism but is openly signaling how it could join.
