## Actor-By-Actor Review

### United States

- **What was forecast:** Reinforcement; bargain and extend vs striking Iranian power plants; international Hormuz framing with weak coalition conversion; no ground invasion.
- **What happened:** Marines and carrier movement; repeated energy/bridge threats; prime-time address (April 1) with 2–3 week “hard” strike framing; Trump Hormuz burden-shifting (“not for us,” allies “go get your own oil”); White House claimed private Iranian flexibility; April 2 bridge strike and April 3 F-15 loss + CSAR; Hegseth removed Army chief and two other generals.
- **What was missed:** **Manned aircraft loss** as a discrete escalation marker; **Pentagon leadership shakeup** timing; **IRGC corporate targeting** doctrine against U.S. firms; extent of **non-U.S.** Hormuz diplomacy as the visible multilateral track.

### Israel

- **What was forecast:** High strike tempo; widening sensitive targets; active Lebanon without replacing Hormuz core; autonomy from U.S. talks.
- **What happened:** Sustained strikes including Tehran power stress, Isfahan-area explosions, pharmaceutical/industrial controversy, Lebanon expansion and heavy casualties, drone strike near worshippers (April 3).
- **What was missed:** Little explicit forecast weight on **holiday-timed symbolic infrastructure** (bridge) as a **civilian optics** accelerant—visible in briefs but not a named prediction object.

### Iran

- **What was forecast:** Selective Hormuz; negotiation denial; sustained strike capacity; retaliation to Israeli widening.
- **What happened:** Gulf energy and utility hits; IRGC/official threats (universities, U.S. companies); Pezeshkian public letter; Oman channel signaling; downing of U.S. fighter; Qalibaf mockery of U.S. narrative.
- **What was missed:** **Named U.S. firm list** and timing ultimatum (April 1) as a **new non-kinetic escalation vector**; **Fars-sourced** 48h ceasefire rejection as a datapoint on **tactical pause** refusal.

### Hezbollah

- **What was forecast:** Active-bounded Lebanon; surge latent unless extreme Israeli-Iran shocks.
- **What happened:** Rocket pressure, Israeli buffer expansion, high casualties, UNIFIL risk.
- **What was missed:** Forecast did not err on absence of activity; main gap is **parallel** Houthi activation reducing Lebanon’s share of “widening” attention.

### Hashd

- **What was forecast:** Sporadic/latent anti-U.S. channel; activates on symbolic strikes or U.S. casualties.
- **What happened:** Journalist kidnapping attributed to Kata’ib Hezbollah (March 31); not the dominant widening mechanism.
- **What was missed:** None major for this actor; kidnapping fit “pressure channel” without making Iraq the lead front.

### Ansar Allah / Houthis

- **What was forecast:** Secondary to Hormuz; maritime attribution conditional; latent if multinational maritime campaign or Iran seeks lane beyond Hormuz.
- **What happened:** March 28 claimed ballistic strike toward Israel; continued Yemen-origin drones; Red Sea as **second chokepoint** narrative in AP/Reuters.
- **What was missed:** Treat **end-of-prior-window signaling** as enough to raise **near-term activation probability** above “secondary” for the **next** week when entry conditions are already partially visible.

## Why The Forecast Missed

- **Under-weighted signals:** Late-prior-week Houthi **entry-condition signaling** treated as latent; briefs showed **operational follow-through on day one** of the window.
- **Under-weighted signals:** European and **consumer-state** capacity to organize **without U.S. leadership** on Hormuz once Washington publicly externalized strait responsibility.
- **Over-weighted framing:** “Hormuz coalition struggles” mapped implicitly to **U.S.-led** formation; the week’s innovation was **fragmented** initiatives (Pakistan, Oman, U.K., China–Pakistan five points).
- **Timing vs logic:** U.S. **energy-strike** execution lagged threats—correct baseline—but **rhetorical and infrastructure escalation** (bridges, power plants as stated targets) still intensified; distinguishing **executed** vs **threatened** widening matters for scorekeeping.
- **Actor-control:** Houthi action plausibly **networked** with the wider axis but behaved as a **semi-autonomous** widening instrument earlier than the “conditional escalator” table suggested.
