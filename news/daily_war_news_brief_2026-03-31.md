# Daily War News Brief - 2026-03-31

## Scope

- Date covered: 2026-03-31
- Timezone standard used: UTC-based publication timing from reviewed sources; some Gulf and wire updates reference local times or “early” next-calendar-day windows as reported
- Focus: Iran / U.S. / Israel conflict and directly connected regional developments

## Executive Summary

- **Oil and shipping dominated the economic-war picture**: front-month **Brent (May)** closed sharply higher while **June Brent** and **WTI** slipped on the same session, as traders weighed **deal rumors** against **ongoing Gulf tanker and port risk**; **March** still printed a historic crude rally in wire and market reporting.
- **President Trump** told allies to **“go get your own oil”** and said securing the **Strait of Hormuz** was **“not for us,”** shifting public burden-sharing rhetoric while the White House announced a **prime-time national address** on the war for the following evening.
- **U.S. and Israeli strikes** continued across **Tehran, Isfahan, Karaj**, and other locations; reporting described **large secondary explosions** near Isfahan and strikes on **industrial, nuclear-related, petrochemical, and university-linked** sites, alongside Israeli claims about a **pharmaceutical plant** Iran framed as civilian medical production.
- **Iran and proxies** remained active in the **Gulf**: a **Kuwait-flagged crude tanker** was hit off **Dubai**, with **Kuwait Petroleum Corporation** and **UAE** authorities describing a **drone attack** and fire; regional reporting also described **interceptions** and **injuries from falling debris** in **Saudi Arabia, UAE, Qatar, Bahrain**, and other areas.
- **Escalation in rhetoric**: **IRGC-linked messaging** threatened **U.S. companies** in the region **from April 1**; the **White House** responded that U.S. forces were prepared to **curtail attacks**, citing a claimed **~90% drop** in Iranian ballistic missile and drone volume since the war’s start.
- **Third-party diplomacy**: **Qatar** warned Iranian strikes had crossed **“many red lines”** and urged avoiding attacks on **nuclear and energy infrastructure**; **Pakistan and China** published a **five-point** de-escalation initiative, while **Hegseth** said talks continued but **military escalation** remained on the table.
