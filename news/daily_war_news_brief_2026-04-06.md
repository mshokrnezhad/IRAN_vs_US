# Daily War News Brief - 2026-04-06

## Scope

- Date covered: 2026-04-06
- Timezone standard used: Wire datelines and **Monday** (**EDT**/U.S. political day) as in **AP**/**Reuters**; some items reference **Tuesday 2026-04-07** as the next deadline-boundary (explicit in **Trump** remarks).
- Focus: Iran / U.S. / Israel conflict and directly connected regional developments

## Executive Summary

- **Mediation package circulated**: **Reuters** (sourcing) described a **Pakistan**-brokered two-stage framework—the **“Islamabad Accord”**—with an immediate **ceasefire** and **Strait of Hormuz** reopening, then **15–20** days to finalize a broader deal including **nuclear** commitments and **sanctions relief**; **Field Marshal Asim Munir** was said to have been in overnight contact with **JD Vance**, **Steve Witkoff**, and **Abbas Araqchi**. **Pakistani** sources told **Reuters** **Iran** had **not** yet committed.
- **Iran**’s **Foreign Ministry** spokesperson **Esmaeil Baghaei** said Tehran had **formulated** its response to ceasefire ideas conveyed via intermediaries, called talks **“incompatible with ultimatums and threats to commit war crimes,”** and reiterated rejection of **“excessive”** prior U.S. demands including a **15-point** plan (**Reuters**).
- **AP** reported **Iran** rejected a **45-day** ceasefire framing and wanted a **permanent** end to the war with **non-reattack guarantees**; **IRNA** was cited for a **10-point** counterproposal via **Pakistan**; **Mojtaba Ferdousi Pour** (head of **Iran**’s **Cairo** mission) told **AP** Tehran would only accept an end of war with guarantees and cited **lost trust** after prior U.S. strikes during talks.
- **Trump** intensified **coercion** language ahead of a **Tuesday 8 p.m. EDT** deadline: **AP** quoted him threatening **all** Iranian **power plants** and **bridges**, including **“The entire country can be taken out in one night, and that night might be tomorrow night”** and **“Every bridge in Iran will be decimated by 12 o’clock tomorrow night”**; he dismissed **war-crimes** concerns (**“No, not at all”**). **U.N.** spokesperson **Stéphane Dujarric** warned attacks on **civilian infrastructure** violate international law (**AP**).
- **Israel** struck **South Pars**/**Asaluyeh** **petrochemical** targets again; **Defense Minister Israel Katz** and **PM Netanyahu** framed the hit as degrading **IRGC** revenue (**AP**). **Iranian** state media said **IRGC** intelligence chief **Maj. Gen. Majid Khademi** was killed; **Israel** also claimed **Quds Force** figure **Asghar Bakeri** killed (**AP**). **Israel** reported overnight strikes on **three Tehran-area airports** and hits near **Sharif University of Technology**; **Iranian** authorities/**state media** reported **at least 29** killed nationwide from strikes (**AP**).
- **Japan**’s government said a **Japanese** national **detained in Iran since January** was **released on bail** and **confirmed Monday**; **Chief Cabinet Secretary Minoru Kihara** demanded full release (**AP**).
