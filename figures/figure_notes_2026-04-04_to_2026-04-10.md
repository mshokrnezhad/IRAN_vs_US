## Coding Assumptions
- Scores are **judgmental indices** (0–3) from wire summaries in the briefs, not verified battle damage assessments.
- **April 7** is coded as a **sharp pivot**: high **U.S.** and **Iranian** rhetoric and kinetic signals **before** the announced **two-week** pause; **diplomatic_activity** rises on that day because formal acceptance and mediation threads dominated headlines by evening.
- **April 8–10** reflect **decoupled axes**: **U.S.–Iran** vertical often **diplomacy-forward** while **Israel–Hezbollah–Lebanon** shows **high military** and **proxy** scores; this is intentional and matches the weekly pattern write-up.
- **Strait** and **throughput** narratives are scored under **maritime_pressure** where reporting emphasized **access** or **closure** claims, without treating every shipper quote as ground truth.
- **Hashd** and **Houthis** stay **near zero** most days because the reviewed brief emphasis was **Hormuz**, **Iran vertical**, and **Lebanon**; absence in the table is **not** proof of zero activity elsewhere.
- **Weekly averages** are simple means across seven days per actor.

## Figure List

### Escalation Intensity
- **What it shows:** Daily sums of weighted `EscalationPressure` and summed `diplomatic_activity_score` across all six actors (script formula).
- **Main finding:** **Peak pressure** around **April 4–6** gives way to a **partial** **dip** after **April 7** on the escalation index while **diplomacy** remains **elevated**—consistent with **pause-plus-parallel-war** dynamics.

### Actor Behavior Heatmap
- **What it shows:** Separate heatmaps for military, diplomacy, proxy, infrastructure, and maritime by actor and date.
- **Main finding:** **Israel** and **Iran** retain **strong military/infrastructure coloring** early week; **U.S.** **diplomacy** darkens **late** week; **Hezbollah** **proxy** intensifies **April 8–9**.

### Geographic Conflict Map
- **What it shows:** Schematic theater nodes, actor placement by `primary_theater`, and stylized pressure arrows (script defaults scaled by weekly summary pressure).
- **Main finding:** **Multi-theater** U.S. and **Gulf/Hormuz**-centered **Iran** coexist with **Lebanon**-heavy **Israel**/**Hezbollah** pressure paths.

### Trigger-Response Flow
- **What it shows:** Top weighted trigger/response pairs from `visual_coding` (non-empty fields, sorted by military + infrastructure + maritime weight).
- **Main finding:** Surfaces **deadline–ultimatum–pause** and **infrastructure/maritime** coercion threads more than a single negotiation chain.

### Scenario Tree
- **What it shows:** Three branches with probability parsed from the **first** `probability_band` of each scenario in `forecast_objects_2026-04-11_to_2026-04-17.csv` (**~42% / ~34% / ~24%**, matching `prediction_2026-04-11_to_2026-04-17.md`).
- **Main finding:** Central mass is **dual-axis truce** (Islamabad + coercive Hormuz + continuing Lebanon fight); **escalatory** mass stresses **talks rupture**, **strait/GCC** surges, and **Lebanon** mass-casualty risk.

### Forecast Risk Matrix
- **What it shows:** Each forecast object by parsed probability band vs impact level, colored by confidence.
- **Main finding:** **Critical** risks cluster under **escalatory** and **most-likely** rows that still carry **high** strategic impact (e.g. offensive restart, GCC utility shock).

### Signals Dashboard
- **What it shows:** Tabular **main_trigger**-as-signal view (first ten rows), scenario tags, invalidators.
- **Main finding:** Watch list emphasizes **Islamabad seating**, **strait metrics**, **public deal-off rhetoric**, and **Lebanon** tempo vs **U.S.–Iran** pause.

## Evidence Limits
- Figures do not prove sortie counts, missile inventory, or correct attribution for every strike or shipping claim.
- Scenario weights in the prediction file are **analytic judgments**; CSV numeric bands are chosen so the renderer’s scenario-level parse matches **42/34/24** on the **first row** of each scenario group.
- Geographic map is **schematic**, not a strike plot.
