## Coding Assumptions
- Scores are **judgmental indices** (0–3) from wire summaries in the briefs, **not** verified battle damage assessments.
- Where attribution was **disputed** (tanker strikes, pharmaceutical plant framing), **infrastructure** or **military** scores reflect **reported** claims and **observed** political effects, not settled forensic truth.
- **Houthi** scores treat **claimed** and **intercept-reported** activity as **operational** signals at **partial** confidence on some days.
- **Hashd** remains **mostly zero** except the **March 31** kidnapping allegation row, coded as **proxy** and **diplomatic** pressure without confirming the underlying crime narrative.
- **Diplomatic_activity** can stay **high** for the U.S. even when outcomes are not de-escalatory, per instruction not to collapse diplomacy into automatic calm.
- **Weekly averages** are simple means across seven days for each actor.

## Figure List

### Escalation Intensity
- **What it shows:** Daily sums of the weighted `EscalationPressure` index and summed `diplomatic_activity_score` across all six actors.
- **Main finding:** Pressure **climbs** through the week with **diplomacy elevated mid-window** (Pakistan/U.K./Oman tracks) **without** a sustained dip in the escalation index—consistent with **coercive endurance war**.

### Actor Behavior Heatmap
- **What it shows:** Separate heatmaps for military, diplomacy, proxy, infrastructure, and maritime scores by actor and date.
- **Main finding:** **Iran** and **US** show **sustained** military and maritime/infrastructure-relevant coloring; **Houthis** spike **proxy** and **maritime** early then **fade** relative to **Gulf** headlines.

### Geographic Conflict Map
- **What it shows:** Theater nodes with actor placement by `primary_theater` and stylized pressure arrows (script defaults layered with weekly pressure scores).
- **Main finding:** The week centers **Iran** and **Gulf/Hormuz** connectivity with **Israel** and **Lebanon** as **parallel** high-pressure anchors; **Houthis** tie to **Yemen/Red Sea**.

### Trigger-Response Flow
- **What it shows:** Top weighted **trigger/response** pairs drawn from `visual_coding` rows that have non-empty triggers and responses.
- **Main finding:** Highlights **infrastructure and maritime coercion loops** (strikes, Hormuz rhetoric, Gulf hits) more than a single clean negotiation breakthrough chain.

### Scenario Tree
- **What it shows:** Three branches (**Most likely ~48%**, **Escalatory ~37%**, **De-escalatory ~15%**) with sample behaviors and triggers from `forecast_objects_2026-04-04_to_2026-04-10.csv`.
- **Main finding:** **Attritional continuity** is the central mass; **escalatory** mass concentrates on **infrastructure acceleration**, **air losses**, and **GCC utility/refinery** shocks.

### Forecast Risk Matrix
- **What it shows:** Each forecast object plotted by **parsed probability band** (numeric ranges averaged) vs **impact level**, colored by **confidence**.
- **Main finding:** **High-impact** items span **all three scenarios**; several **Critical** risks sit in the **escalatory** cluster while **de-escalatory** outcomes require **rare** behavioral alignment.

### Signals Dashboard
- **What it shows:** Tabular view of **main triggers** (used as **signals**) with scenario tags and invalidators.
- **Main finding:** Monitoring emphasis falls on **strike tempo**, **Hormuz implementation text**, **manned aircraft/CSAR**, and **GCC refinery/desalination** continuity.

## Evidence Limits
- Figures **do not prove** order-of-battle accuracy, **true** missile inventory, or **correct** attribution for every **tanker** or **refinery** event.
- **Scenario probabilities** in the prediction file are **analytic judgments**; the CSV uses **numeric bands** compatible with the renderer and **approximates** the 48/37/15 split on the **first row** of each scenario group.
- **Geographic map** is **schematic**, not a strike plot.
